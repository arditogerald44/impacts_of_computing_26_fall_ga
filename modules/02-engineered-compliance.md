## 🧠 1. Theoretical Context: EdTech as Behavioral Engineering

This week, we bridge the gap between commercial attention-capture mechanics and the infrastructure of higher education. While B.J. Fogg outlines the modern formula for corporate behavior manipulation ($B=MAP$), Audrey Watters exposes how modern Educational Technology (EdTech) frameworks are rooted directly in the behaviorist mechanics of B.F. Skinner.

Modern tracking portals, automated plagiarism checkers, and remote algorithmic proctoring systems are marketed using the language of efficiency and personalization. However, as Watters notes, their codebases are engineered to monitor click telemetry, track user cadence, and nudge students into predictable execution channels. 

This mirrors the Vatican’s warning in *Antiqua et nova* regarding **"Rapidification"** (§67)—the systematic acceleration of workplace tasks driven by automated software that deskills the human operator and forces them to adapt to the frantic pace of an opaque machine pipeline.
